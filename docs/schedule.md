# Space Schedule — Artemis II → Artemis III

**Window:** 2026-04-11 (Artemis II splashdown) → Artemis III launch (NET mid-2027)

The next ~14 months are defined by one question: *can the Artemis III stack actually be ready?* That means HLS, Starship refilling, new suits, and Orion post-flight certification all have to converge. Every other mission below is either (a) on the Artemis III critical path, (b) a science flagship with its own hard launch window, or (c) a geopolitical flag-planting attempt at the lunar south pole.

Dates are NET (no-earlier-than) and will slip. This file is the source of truth for the terminal tracker's "next up" section.

---

## On the Artemis III critical path

| Date (NET) | Event | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Q2 2026 | **Artemis II post-flight review** | NASA certifies Orion heat shield performance, life support, nav. If any anomaly surfaces, Artemis III slips. |
| Q2–Q3 2026 | **Starship orbital propellant transfer demo** | Hard gate. No refilling, no HLS. SpaceX must show ship-to-ship transfer in LEO at mission-representative scale. |
| Q3–Q4 2026 | **Axiom AxEMU suit qualification** | Lunar EVA suit must pass thermal/vacuum/dust testing before crew rotation locks. |
| Q4 2026 – Q1 2027 | **Uncrewed Starship HLS lunar demo landing** | Contractually required before crew flies on HLS. The biggest single de-risking event of the whole campaign. |
| Q1–Q2 2027 | **Artemis III crew training lockout + stack integration** | SLS Block 1 Core Stage 3, Orion, HLS, Gateway (cancelled scope), suits — all converge at KSC. |
| **NET mid-2027** | **🌕 Artemis III launch** | First crewed lunar landing since Apollo 17 (Dec 1972). First woman, first person of color on the Moon. South pole. |
